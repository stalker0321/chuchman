Ways of transmission of coronavirus

By airborne droplets

The micro-droplets of water that your neighbor infected with the coronavirus emits has a very high chance of infecting you, so be sure to wear a mask!

Through surfaces

ITry to avoid touching the surface, as on metal and plastic, the virus can survive for up to 2-3 days.

Through blood

Like most other viruses, coronavirus can be transmitted through various body fluids. These are blood and saliva. Infection can occur during the reuse of medical instruments without prior disinfection.

Through cats

Never come into contact with stray cats, as it is scientifically proven that cats are carriers of the coronavirus.

Symptoms of the coronavirus

Be sure to tell your parents if you find any of these symptoms!

Increased body temperature

Dry cough

Prolonged fatigue

Also, infection with coronavirus can be accompanied by the following symptoms:

- sore throat

- headache

- loss of smell and taste

Prevention of coronavirus

Never forget a few simple rules and it will be much more difficult for the coronavirus to infect you.

Hygiene

Always wash my hands when coming from the street or before eating. A lot of microbes accumulate on the hands and coronavirus is not an exception.

Avoid contact with sick people

Try not to communicate with people who have symptoms of coronavirus, as it is very easy to get infected.

Food processing

It is imperative to wash not only hands, but also food. On frozen food, the coronavirus can last 2-3 months!

Stick to self-isolation

Try to stay at home as much as possible, so you have much less chance of getting infected.
