Coronavirus history

Appearance versions

    There are several versions of how the coronavirus infection appeared and spread throughout the planet. Let's start with the fact that the first case was registered in China, in the city of Wuhan. There is a large market in the city, which sells various delicacies, beloved by the Chinese.

    The first version says that the coronavirus originated among animals and already on the market itself, through the transmission of infection between animals, mutated and transmitted to humans. The Chinese government also adheres to this version.

     The second version lies in the accusation of the staff of the IIV - Wuhan Institute of Virology. The institute is located close to the market where the first cases of the disease were diagnosed, so it was believed that there was a leak of biological material from the laboratory, which led to a pandemic in China, and then in the world.

     Some are of the opinion that all this is a worldwide conspiracy and an attempt to artificially reduce the world's population, but these are only guesses, not confirmed by real facts.

First cases

The first diagnosis was made as early as December 31, 2019, while people with very similar symptoms have been hospitalized since December 8 of the same year. From January 1, 2020, the authorities began to introduce quarantine, first local, then widespread. Two weeks later, the Chinese authorities admitted that they had failed to contain the virus, despite the security measures taken. Many countries have introduced mandatory temperature controls when entering the country. By the end of the month, the virus was seen in many parts of the world, which allowed the WHO to declare the SARC-COV-2 pandemic.

Ways of transmission of coronavirus

By airborne droplets

Microdroplets of water, which literally erupt from a patient when sneezing, contain a coronavirus infection. Breathing in such air, a person will be almost 100% infected

Through surfaces

It has been proven that the virus can live on the surfaces of various objects for up to several hours. On metal and plastic, the virus can survive for up to 2-3 days. This allows the coronavirus to spread even through handshakes.

Through blood

Like most other viruses, coronavirus can be transmitted through various body fluids. These are blood and saliva. Infection can occur during the reuse of medical instruments without prior disinfection.

Through cats

It has been proven that the feline family can be infected with the coronavirus and also transmit it to the rest of its species and also to humans. It is recommended to avoid contact with stray cats.

Complications of coronavirus

What is the result of the "protracted" coronavirus?

In most cases, the disease is mild to moderate, but in some cases, the coronavirus causes severe inflammation, which can ultimately lead to fatal pneumonia. Due to its long presence in the human body, the virus causes inflammatory processes. The following systems are mainly affected:

- cardiovascular (acute heart failure, disseminated intravascular coagulation);

- urinary system (acute renal failure);

- respiratory system (acute respiratory distress syndrome, acute respiratory failure).

Prevention of coronavirus

To avoid getting infected with the coronavirus, follow these simple rules:

Hand treatment

We constantly touch with our hands to many surfaces on which a virus can inhabit, and then we touch our face, nose, lips, etc. The virus enters the mucous membrane and the person becomes infected. To prevent this situation, you need to touch your face with your hands as little as possible and treat your hands as often as possible with soap or an antiseptic.

Avoid contact with people with signs of SARS

If you are forced to leave your home, then you need to avoid contact with people who have signs of SARS. It is worth bypassing sneezing / coughing people on the streets, keeping social media. distance and, of course, put on a mask, preferably when leaving the house.

Food processing

If you are going to consume products that were not packaged in the store, then they should be heat treated or treated with water. For example, for fruits, rinsing over hot water will be enough, but raw meat or raw eggs must be boiled or fried.

Stick to self-isolation

The simplest rule is to minimize the time spent on the street. This will help to avoid infection from strangers by airborne droplets. You need to refrain from walking, limiting yourself only to trips to work, school or to the store.
